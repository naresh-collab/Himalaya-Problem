key findings from the bucket data

39,889 UPI-only users (38.7%) made exactly 1 UPI txn and have 0% D30-60. These are not dormant users — they are gone. Every re-engagement rupee spent here has near-zero ROI. Redirect budget to the 6+ bucket instead.
33,134 UPI-only users with 6+ txns have 41% D30-60 — the highest retention of any UPI-only sub-segment by 3×. They're engaged but mono-service. A RuPay or Shop nudge to this cohort alone could unlock 18,000+ retained D30-60 users.
UPI → RuPay bucket 0 = 6,293 users (78.4%) who adopted RuPay on the same session as first UPI, with 35.2% D30-60. Users who took even 1 UPI txn before RuPay (bucket 1) jump to 49.9% D30-60. A post-transaction RuPay prompt doubles retention.
UPI → RCBP is almost exclusively a power-user behavior: 69.9% of RCBP adopters had 6+ UPI txns before their first RCBP. 89.4% D30-60 for that sub-segment. RCBP is the natural endpoint of high-frequency UPI users.
Across every single path, bucket 6+ is the highest-retention sub-segment. UPI txn frequency before next step is the strongest predictor of long-term retention — stronger than the path itself.

actionables — from bucket data + all prior analyses

1
39,889 UPI-only, 1-txn users — stop spending, start understanding
0% D30-60. These users did not retain. They are not a re-engagement opportunity
Exception: if transaction was D27–30 days ago, a final same-session win-back offer is still viable — but only in the first 72h
Root-cause analysis needed: was this 1 txn a failed P2P send? A bill? A merchant they never revisited? Understanding the context determines if this is a product or acquisition problem
Stop sending D7 and D14 re-engagement push campaigns to this segment — CTRs will be near zero and you're burning notification quota that affects deliverability for other segments
2
33,134 UPI-only, 6+ txns — the highest-value cross-sell pool in the cohort
41% D30-60 already — highest retention of any UPI-only sub-segment
These users have proven payment frequency. They need a reason to expand, not a reason to transact
Target with: RuPay (P80 window = 1d, so run as in-app banner on next login), Shop wave 2 (D20–25 window), POPcard (same-session CTA)
If 20% of 33k convert to RuPay → 6,600 users move to UPI→RuPay path at 35.9% D30-60. Net lift = ~1,400 additional retained users just from this segment
Priority comms: personalised — "You've made X payments this month. Unlock higher rewards with RuPay" — include their actual transaction count
3
UPI txn frequency as retention signal — use it to score users in real time
Across every path, the 6+ bucket retains at 40–100% D30-60. Frequency predicts retention better than which service a user adopted
Build a "UPI frequency score" (txn count in D0-7) — this is already your activation metric (5+ txns). Use 3+ as "at-risk engaged" and 6+ as "power user"
Power users (6+ txns) need expansion triggers. At-risk (3–5 txns) need habit reinforcement (streak rewards, cashback ladders). Low (1–2 txns) need UPI habit formation before any cross-sell
Don't push Shop or RCBP to bucket-1 or bucket-2 users — their UPI habit isn't formed yet and cross-sell noise will increase churn, not reduce it
4
RuPay same-session adoption (78.4%) — redesign the post-txn screen now
The bucket-0 UPI → RuPay data confirms: most RuPay decisions happen before the user's second UPI transaction
Bucket-1 RuPay users (1 UPI txn before RuPay) have 49.9% D30-60 vs 35.2% for bucket-0. A single completed UPI txn before the RuPay prompt doubles retention lift
Implication: show the RuPay CTA AFTER the first transaction success screen, not before or during. The completed payment creates the psychological anchor ("this works, now improve it")
For bucket-6+ RuPay users (240 users, 74.2% D30-60): these are habituated UPI users who finally linked RuPay late. They are already committed — focus cross-sell on Shop and RCBP
5
RCBP is a power-user endpoint — don't push it early
69.9% of UPI→RCBP users had 6+ UPI txns before RCBP. Only 4.4% had 0. RCBP naturally follows deep UPI engagement — it is NOT an early-funnel product
89.4% D30-60 for 6+ bucket RCBP users — these are your best customers. Treat them as VIPs, not just conversion targets
Don't run RCBP campaigns to users with <3 UPI txns. These users haven't formed the UPI habit; RCBP messaging will confuse them and waste budget
Trigger: when a user crosses the 6-txn threshold AND their recent txns include a mobile network (Jio/Airtel payee name), fire an RCBP contextual in-app message within 24h
UPI → RCBP → Shop = 96.7–98.1% D30-60. After first RCBP, push Shop at RCBP success screen — this is your highest-conversion moment for the Shop funnel
6
Shop — only target users with 3+ UPI txns before nudging
UPI→Shop bucket-0 (0 UPI txns before Shop, 15.3% of path): 27.6% D30-60. Bucket-6+: 73.4%. D30-60 triples from lowest to highest bucket
These bucket-0 shop users likely came through a Shop-first flow or campaign — they haven't habituated to UPI yet, so shop order doesn't lock them in
Gate Shop campaigns behind a 3+ UPI txn requirement. Users with 3+ UPI txns before their first shop order retain at 47–52% vs 28–40% for earlier buckets
This also reduces CAC on shop — users who are already engaged cost less to convert and generate more long-term GMV. Focus Shop acquisition spend on existing UPI transactors, not cold installs
Shop → UPI path (195 users, 100% in bucket 0 with 1 P80 shop order): only 33.2% D30-60. Confirms again — shop-first doesn't build lasting retention. UPI must come first
